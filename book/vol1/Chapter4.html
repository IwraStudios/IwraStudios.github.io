Chapter IV

Morning came and as usual nothing planned for the day. I got up, yelled downstairs to see if my mom was home, which of course she wasn’t, and jumped in to some quick clothes for a simple day.

My “quick” clothes, or all wearables for that matter, consists of a blouse and long pants. Note that I would never wear any T-shirts without being forced to. I always wore blouses or polo’s and never any slack off clothes. I dressed myself as you would say rather “pishposh”. Just as extra information.

When I got downstairs it was already 12 and the world looked alive, well as far as a small village could look alive. If you would observe a free day, when it comes to me you would’ve been bored in the first seven seconds. Back then, as I already explained, I didn’t do much. It’s pretty much me trying so hard not to kill myself from boredom or starvation. Some simple bites, a game which changes continuously and browsing some internet now and then. Around 5’o’clock I got up from my computer to get some food ready. I was thinking to myself ”Another useless day where in nothing happens”. When I got downstairs I sat on the piano stool, like usually, but when I looked on my phone I saw that my wifi connection didn’t work, and it hasn’t worked since this morning. After a quick fix I immediately got 18 messages, which was to be expected after a long day of doing absolutely nothing. 10 of those were from a boring group I got forced into, 4 of those were single messages of people asking if I could come online on Skype. And 4 were from the person. One was in the morning where it said:

(Unknown Caller)

Barbeque chillings from the guys
Remember?

I had no clue of any barbeque and certainly not one I got invited to.

A couple of minutes earlier before fixing my WiFi I got:

He where you at??
Oh wait never mind, I see you

Swiftly I looked around for someone who may approach me. After a look around I felt stupid, because I was standing inside my own house and no one was home. I had no idea what was going on, did this guy just understand that I wasn’t on that barbeque? Or did I actually have a barbeque invitation from someone I forgot about. Confused I started typing

Uhmm dude, I think you have the wrong guy. I am not on any barbe…

Bluntly interrupted he came online and said:

Who are you!?
